# Hormozi Principles → Executable Rules

Source: Alex Hormozi's personal-brand talk + the "6 content shifts" teardown (35,000 posts, $4M, 40 months, 7.8M followers).
Purpose: deterministic rules our **LinkedIn agent** can follow and our **human team lead** can govern with.

> Translation note: Hormozi's data is mostly YouTube/multi-platform. Every rule below is restated for **Puru Kathuria on LinkedIn**, whose ideal customer = engineering students/professionals/leaders + AI-engineering professionals (the ICPs in `linkedin/LINKEDIN-SOP.md`). Where a principle is platform-specific, the LinkedIn translation is marked **[LI]**.

---

## PART A — THE ONE DEFINITION (everything hangs off this)

**Branding = the deliberate pairing of [your thing] with [something the ideal customer likes] through an outcome.**

- Advertising = letting people know about your stuff. Branding = the *pairing* that occurs as a result. They are different. You can have good advertising + bad branding (Bud Light × Dylan Mulvaney: lots of reach, wrong pairing, lost money).
- Branding ALWAYS happens. Our job is **good** branding = pairing our thing with **good outcomes for our *ideal* customer** (not everyone).
- "Good vs bad" is **objective**: did the pairing net more money / more of the right people, or fewer? Not opinion.
- **Brand = the association.** Scatter the flowers and there is no bouquet. The brand only exists in the deliberate, repeated pairing.

**Executable test for the agent — every post must answer YES:**
1. What good outcome is this post pairing Puru with? (e.g. "you shipped 2x with AI", "you broke into AI-native eng")
2. Does our *ideal* ICP like that outcome? (name the ICP)
3. Are we pairing *toward* us, not away? (no pairing the brand with something our ICP dislikes)

If any answer is no/unknown → **do not post.**

## PART B — THE THREE BRAND METRICS (how we measure brand, deterministically)

Hormozi operationalizes brand as 3 measurable dials:

| Metric | Definition | LinkedIn proxy [LI] |
|--------|-----------|---------------------|
| **Influence** | Does seeing the brand change behavior at all? (any reaction = recognition) | comments, saves, reshares, profile clicks, DMs |
| **Direction** | Toward us or away? | sentiment of comments; follows vs unfollows; DMs that ask to work with us |
| **Reach** | How many people it changes for | impressions, unique reach, follower delta |

Rules:
- A strong brand is **NOT required to be polar** (loved + hated). Taylor Swift / Mother Teresa / Apple = large, strong, *positive*. **We build a strong POSITIVE brand. We do not manufacture controversy for reach.**
- Every new pairing (new topic, new format, new collab) is a **bet**: you lose some audience, you gain more. Ship it if expected net (reach × positive direction) is up. *"Don't let 5 mean comments stop you from gaining 500 new people who like the new thing."*
- Small-audience + high-influence still counts (your parents have huge influence, tiny reach). A 5,000-follower account with the *right* followers can out-earn a 1M general account.

## PART C — THE SIX CONTENT SHIFTS (the core operating doctrine)

Each is a default the agent follows unless the team lead overrides with data.

### 1. Edutainment → **Education**
- Three content types by *most common outcome*: **Entertainment** (point = get watched), **Education** (point = change behavior), **Edutainment** (both).
- "Learning = same condition, new behavior." If the content doesn't change what the viewer *does*, it didn't educate.
- **All views are not equal.** The funnel myth ("entertainment viewers graduate to education/customers") is false in his data: entertainment viewers want more entertainment; education viewers want more education; customers buy. They do **not** convert into each other at a rate you can build a business on.
- **Rule [LI]:** Puru posts to *change the engineer's behavior* (teach a tactic, shift a belief, prompt an action). We do NOT chase entertaining/viral-for-its-own-sake posts. Our people self-educate; serve them.

### 2. For us → **For you**
- The trap: making content for *the team* ("team, what should we post?") instead of the ideal customer. Symptom: your actual ICP says "it stopped resonating."
- **Rule:** Every post is made for the **business owner / engineer ICP**, never for internal taste. Before publishing, the agent states the single named ICP the post serves.
- Five tactical "for you" levers (translated **[LI]**):
  1. **Packaging: vague → clear.** The hook/first line must say *exactly* what the reader gets. No curiosity-gap bait. "If they want the thing, they click; then give them exactly that thing."
  2. **Introductions: confirm → proof.** Education requires you *edify why they should listen* (proof), not just restate the headline. (See PART D: Proof-Promise-Plan.)
  3. **Meat: razzle → list, steps, stories.** Emphasize **language/message over production.** In his data, ~75% of top posts emphasized clear language; ~75% of worst emphasized effects.
  4. **Visuals: overproduction → effective production.** Keep only visuals that *aid understanding* (data, charts, words-on-screen). Cut anything that distracts (changing backgrounds, wiz-bangs). For us [LI]: clean carousels/diagrams that clarify > flashy graphics. (Ref: `linkedin/burry-diagram` hand-drawn pipeline.)
  5. **Pre-work: post-production → pre-search.** "An ounce of pre-work is worth a pound of post." Research/outline *before*; near-zero editing after → 4x output at higher quality.

### 3. Wide → **Narrow**
- People who watch relationships/food/lifestyle want *more of that* — they are not your business owners. Narrow to the topic only your ICP wants.
- **Rule [LI]:** Puru's lane = **AI-native software engineering** (craft, career, vision). Every post has "engineering/AI" in its spine. Narrow to double down; only branch to adjacent topics deliberately to widen reach, never randomly. **Distant/random pairings hurt the brand** (hard to associate). Curate the garden: grow good flowers, pull weeds.

### 4. Views → **Revenue** (paired metrics)
- Views alone is a vanity/quantity metric. You need a **paired quality metric.** Hormozi switched to **ad revenue = views × RPM**, because RPM encodes *audience quality* (advertisers pay more for business owners). High views + low revenue = wrong people.
- **Principle: minimize latency** between the metric you track and the behavior you want — fast feedback (daily) beats slow (a 6–12mo deal cycle).
- **Paired-KPI rule (use everywhere):** always track a **quantity** metric against a **quality** metric so one can't be gamed (e.g. tickets resolved × resolution rating).
- **Rule [LI] — our paired metric (we are NOT ad-monetized):**
  - Quantity = reach / impressions.
  - Quality = **right-ICP engagement**: reshares + saves + comments-from-engineers + qualified DMs + opt-ins/applications (e.g. Lex/Prof signups, campus-license inquiries).
  - Optimize **reach × right-ICP-engagement**, NOT raw views or likes. *Likes ain't cash. Cash is cash.* A 10k-impression post from $10M+ eng leaders beats a 1M-impression post of the wrong crowd.

### 5. Shorts → **Longs**
- Same false-funnel: shorts viewers want more shorts; longs viewers want more longs. Longs drove the conversions (book sales, opt-ins, applications).
- **Why:** influence comes from **reinforcement cycles** = time spent. 2 hours of longs ≈ 480 fifteen-second shorts of exposure. Longs let you stack Status-Power-Credibility-Likeness.
- Shorts still useful as **top-of-funnel facial recognition / retargeting**, often on a *different* platform, to pull someone into your longs elsewhere.
- **Rule [LI]:** Prioritize **substantive long-form** (text posts that fully teach, carousels, articles, long video) for conversion. Use **short hooks/snippets** for reach + recognition that route to the long stuff. Keep shorts a bit **wider** (philosophy, productivity) since their ROI is recognition + retargeting, not direct conversion.

### 6. Assume more → **Assume nothing**
- Stop assuming the audience knows Puru. Most viewers are seeing him for the first time.
- Tactical rules (the agent enforces all):
  1. **Title/hook like they don't know you.** "Day in the life of Alex Hormozi" → "Day in the life of a $200M/yr CEO." Add the *identifier* a stranger needs. [LI]: lead with the role/credential, not the name ("An ex-Google engineer on…" > "Puru on…").
  2. **Introduce yourself every time** (warm people like the reminder, cold people need it).
  3. **Say why they should listen** every time (proof/credential).
  4. **No unexplained inside jokes / references.** "Inside joke not understood = reader on the outside. People buy from the inside." Fully explain every reference.
  5. **Reintroduce your greatest hits** — new audience hasn't seen last year's best post. Repost it; it often does as well or better (the audience grew). Like a TV theme song / The Simpsons chalkboard: same intro, small variety, serves both new + loyal. (Reinforces `linkedin/linkedin-growth-playbook.md`: recycle save-able templates.)
  6. **Mentally talk to a stranger every time** — if the content works, you are.

## PART D — INTRO FORMULA: PROOF · PROMISE · PLAN

Every long post/video opens with these three (any order; lead with the most important):
- **Proof** — why they should believe you (credential, result, data). For Puru, lead with **proof** (ex-Google / FDE / Lex outcomes) because the reader's first silent question is *"why should I listen to you?"*
- **Promise** — what they'll get/learn.
- **Plan** — what's going to happen / the structure.

Agent rule: no long post ships without an explicit Proof + Promise + Plan in the first ~3 lines.

## PART E — SPCL: THE INFLUENCE STACK

**Influence = likelihood of compliance with a request.** Built from four stackable dials (stack all four for max influence; each works alone):

| Letter | Meaning | How to demonstrate in content |
|--------|---------|-------------------------------|
| **S — Status** | You control scarce resources people want | Show proof of results/access (e.g. "did $X", real company, real audience). |
| **P — Power** | Say-do correspondence: you said X, they did it, a good thing happened → they comply again | Give a tactic the reader can apply that *works*; deliver wins repeatedly. (Hormozi: if he had to pick ONE, pick Power.) |
| **C — Credibility** | Third-party validation, hard-to-fake proof | Cite verifiable proof (results, named orgs, data, "$10M building behind me" type signals). |
| **L — Likeness** | Relatability — shared values/vibe, or literally looks like them | Just be you; lean into what's uniquely Puru. Demographics matter (audience skews like the creator). |

Rules:
- **Influence is domain-specific.** Puru has high SPCL in AI/engineering, ~zero in fashion. Only make requests where his SPCL is high.
- One event can check multiple boxes (a launch = status *and* credibility *and*, if others copy it and win, power).
- The agent should engineer each post to demonstrate at least Status + one of (Power/Credibility), and always carry Likeness.

## PART F — VOLUME, CONTENT-AS-TARGETING, EXPECTATIONS

- **Volume doctrine:** (1) *Anything works better than nothing* — if you haven't posted in 90 days, just post. (2) *Some things work better than others.* (3) *Nothing works forever.* Entrepreneur's job: start → find what works better → do as much of it as possible → until it slows → find the next thing. **"Volume negates luck."**
- **Content IS the targeting.** The algorithm reads your topic, words, even visuals, and shows it to people with a matching watch history. So: *to attract the right avatar, make content for that avatar.* "Social media is becoming interest media."
- **Realistic expectations (market-size math).** Don't compare your views to entertainers'. Size the *real* addressable audience and judge against that. (Hormozi: 9% own businesses; 5% of those >$1M; ~0.4% >$10M.) **[LI] for us:** engineers/AI-engineers are a slice of LinkedIn — a 10k-impression post landing on the right eng leaders is a win; do not benchmark against viral generalist creators.
- **"Don't get the same scar twice."** Mistakes are fine (they're lessons); *repeating* a known mistake is not. Once data says a thing (e.g. "introduce yourself", "high production underperforms"), encode it as a rule and never relapse.
- **Show what only you can show, say what only you can say.** Lead with demonstration/labor, not just claims. Early on you can't out-*outcome* people, but you can **out-work** them ("I made 35,000 posts, here's what I learned" is valuable from anyone). State facts, tell the truth — then you never have to manage claims.

## PART G — PRODUCT/EXPERIENCE > PRE-PURCHASE BRANDING

- Most branding levers act *before* purchase. But after purchase, **the product does most of the branding.** A great ad + bad product = "this sucks," and the hate transfers backward to everyone you paired with.
- Brand can carry a **"good enough"** product the extra distance, but a premium brand on a bad product only wins the *first* sale, never the repeats. (Buffett: a premium brand must deliver something special — *or it won't keep the business.*)
- **[LI] for us:** the "product" = Lex / Prof / the actual teaching. Posts can win the click; the experience must be dialed or the brand erodes. Only make premium claims the product can back.

## PART H — MONETIZING THE AUDIENCE (4 ways, risk/value continuum)

For when the brand is built. Ordered fast/low-value → slow/high-value:
1. **Affiliates** — get paid *after* sales. Fastest, low risk, low exit value, uncapped upside.
2. **Sponsorships** — paid *first* to advertise (CPM). Includes **whitelisting** (they run your post as their ad → they pay to grow *your* brand while converting better). Not very sellable unless you're a media/talent company.
3. **Partnerships** — equity for promotion (minority/majority by leverage). Take **some equity up front** + performance vesting + time-based vesting; association is permanent once public.
4. **Own brand** — start your own product (white-label = fast/cheap/copyable; custom = costlier but defensible pricing power). 100% equity, you pay others down the line.
- These are **not mutually exclusive** (a partnership can include affiliate + sponsorship terms). Balance four levers: **exit value, cash in/out, work required, risk.** More cash now = lean affiliate/sponsorship; belief in upside = lean equity.

## PART I — THE AGENT'S DETERMINISTIC PRE-PUBLISH CHECKLIST

The LinkedIn agent must pass ALL before scheduling a post:

- [ ] **Pairing named:** states the good outcome + the single ICP it pairs Puru with (PART A test).
- [ ] **Education, not entertainment:** the post changes a behavior/belief; states the intended change (Shift 1).
- [ ] **For-you, not for-us:** made for the ICP, not internal taste (Shift 2).
- [ ] **Narrow/on-lane:** sits in AI-native engineering (or a *deliberate* adjacent widen) (Shift 3).
- [ ] **Clear, not vague:** hook says exactly what the reader gets; no curiosity bait (Shift 2.1).
- [ ] **Proof · Promise · Plan** present in the first ~3 lines (PART D).
- [ ] **Assume-nothing:** identifier in hook, self-intro, why-listen, no unexplained inside jokes (Shift 6).
- [ ] **SPCL:** demonstrates Status + (Power or Credibility), carries Likeness (PART E).
- [ ] **Format:** message > production; matches `linkedin/LINKEDIN-SOP.md` shape spec (~150 words, white space, mic-drop, recognizable-face image).
- [ ] **Right metric:** designed for reach × right-ICP-engagement (reshares/saves/qualified DMs), not likes (Shift 4).
- [ ] **Truth:** every stat/quote verified; facts only (PART F).

**Human team lead owns:** the bets (new pairings/topics), reading *between* the comments (not literally), the quality bar, recycling the greatest-hits calendar, and overriding any default with data — then **encoding the result as a new rule so the same scar never happens twice.**

## PART J — ONE-LINE MNEMONICS (pin these)

- Branding = deliberate pairing through an outcome.
- Good branding = pair our thing with good outcomes for our **ideal** customer.
- Influence × Direction × Reach = the brand.
- Educate (change behavior), don't entertain. All views aren't equal.
- Make it **for you** (the ICP), not **for us** (the team).
- **Narrow** to win; random pairings kill brands.
- Track **reach × right-ICP-engagement**, not likes. Likes ain't cash.
- **Longs** convert; shorts get recognized. Reinforcement cycles = time.
- **Assume nothing** — every viewer is new.
- **Proof · Promise · Plan.**
- **S**tatus **P**ower **C**redibility **L**ikeness.
- Volume negates luck. Anything > nothing. Nothing works forever.
- Content is the targeting. Social → interest media.
- Don't get the same scar twice.
- Say what only we can say; show what only we can show.
