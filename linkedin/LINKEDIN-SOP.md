# LinkedIn SOP — Puru Kathuria (Lex AI Labs)

**Goal:** Grow Puru Kathuria into the default thought leader on **AI-native software engineering** — 9,100 → 20,000 followers, then beyond.
**Operating principle:** ONE lane, served at four altitudes. We never drift.

---

## 0. The North Star (read every day)

> *"AI is rewriting what software engineering is. Who survives it, and how?"*

Every ICP cares about this same question at a different altitude:

| ICP | Who | What they want | How we serve |
|-----|-----|----------------|--------------|
| **ICP1** (volume) | Eng undergrads + working devs (5–150L) | Break in / don't get left behind | Craft + Career posts. This ICP drives follower count. |
| **ICP2** | VP/Dir Eng at service & lower-tier product firms | Make their team AI-native, prove it to clients | Craft posts on team productivity, AI-powered SDLC |
| **ICP3** | Leaders at Google/MSFT/Amzn/Sarvam/top labs | (We want to look like) an AI visionary worth collaborating with | Vision/Opinion posts + DMs |
| **ICP4** | VC / Dean / Director / HoD | Campus license, Prof-as-credit, faculty upskilling | Aura from posts + direct outreach |

**Posts win ICP1 at scale. ICP2 follows the craft. ICP3/ICP4 are NOT won by posts — they're won by the cumulative aura PLUS direct DMs/comments. Posts are top-of-funnel; close those in the inbox.**

## 1. The Lane & The 3-Axis Model

**Our lane:** AI-native software engineering — the craft, the career, the vision.
Where Puru has unfair authority: **ex-Google + FDE + building Lex AI Labs.**
We do NOT post: generic AI news commentary (no edge), DSA/interview-prep (saturated, Sameer owns it), motivational fluff.

Every post is tagged on **three orthogonal axes** (plus ICP + hook). The axes don't overlap and nothing escapes them — a draft that can't be tagged on all three is incoherent → kill it. **Axis 1 (Intent) drives topic selection and the weekly mix.**

### Axis 1 — INTENT (the job the post does) — *the primary axis*

| Intent | Job | Lives here | Serves |
|--------|-----|-----------|--------|
| **Teach** | change a behavior | explainers, algorithm explainers, complex-topic explainers, craft how-tos (**← PROF viz, our moat**) | ICP1/2 — conversion engine |
| **Provoke** | shift a belief | contrarian theses, AI-strategy teardowns (NVIDIA, Anthropic), predictions | ICP3/4 — the "AI visionary" aura |
| **Prove** | demonstrate | wins, launches, **partnerships (IILM)**, case studies | SPCL status/credibility, trust |
| **Relate** | humanize | first-person vulnerable, lessons, behind-the-scenes | comments, likeness |
| **Reach** | get seen | memes, hot-news riffs | top-of-funnel recognition **ONLY — thin, capped, never the main act** |

**Weekly Intent target-mix (authority-led):** **Teach 45 / Provoke 30 / Prove 10 / Relate 10 / Reach 5.**
Of ~6 posts/week ≈ Teach ~3, Provoke ~2, Prove ~1/wk, Relate ~1/wk, Reach sprinkled & capped. (Tracked in §8.)

### Axis 2 — TOPIC (subject matter / the old pillars)
**Craft** · **Career/Identity** · **Vision** · **AI-news** · **Personal.** Narrow to win; only widen deliberately (random pairings hurt the brand).

### Axis 3 — FORMAT (mode + asset)
explainer-with-**PROF-viz** · carousel · text · **story-narrative** · meme · screenshot · whiteboard-video.
> Storytelling is a **format available to any intent**, not a category. "News as story" = `Provoke/Reach × AI-news × story`. PROF viz is the **asset that makes a Teach post un-copyable** ("show what only you can show") — not a bucket.

**Rule:** Every post is tagged `Intent × Topic × Format` + `(ONE named ICP)` + `(one hook mold)`. If any axis is unnameable, or the ICP is unnameable, kill it.

## 3. Weekly Rhythm (the anti-inconsistency engine)

Inconsistency is the #1 account killer. The breakout in our benchmark (Sameer) came at **month 8–9** — surviving the flat early stretch IS the skill. Gaps reset that clock.

- **Batch day (e.g. Monday):** Puru records 5–7 voice notes. Writer drafts the week. Designer queues images.
- **Buffer rule:** ALWAYS keep ≥5 finished posts scheduled ahead. A busy week never breaks the streak.
- **Cadence:** ~6 posts/week, **Mon–Sat.** Sunday off or 1 light personal post.
- **Recycling calendar:** re-run a save-able "resource list" template every **2–3 weeks** (the repost engine — see §6).

## 4. Daily Checklist (the writer/scheduler runs this every day)

1. **Pick** one item from the Topic Bank to fill the day's **Intent slot** (follow the week's running tally vs the §1 mix — Teach 45 / Provoke 30 / Prove 10 / Relate 10 / Reach 5; pick whichever Intent is most *behind* target). Confirm its full tag: `Intent × Topic × Format` + `ONE named ICP` + `hook mold`.
2. **Draft** to the Pre-Publish Gate (§5). One idea only.
3. **Hook** — open with a mold from the Hook Library (§7). The first line is 80% of the job.
4. **Image** — attach per §5.B. Never default to text-only.
5. **Run the §5 Pre-Publish Gate** — every box, both layers. Any miss → fix or kill, pick another.
6. **Puru approves** (voice/opinion must ring true).
7. **Schedule** into the buffer slot.
8. **Engage block (20–30 min):** comment on 5–10 ICP3/ICP4 accounts; reply to EVERY comment on our own posts within the first 60 min (comments = strongest algo signal).

## 5. Pre-Publish Gate (hard checklist — non-negotiable)

The agent must pass **EVERY** box below before scheduling. Two layers: **A) Substance** (does the post earn the right to exist) and **B) Format** (does it land). A miss in either layer = kill or fix. Principles behind each gate live in `../hormozi/HORMOZI-PRINCIPLES.md`.

### A. Substance gates (Hormozi)

- [ ] **Pairing named** — states the *good outcome* this post pairs Puru with, and the *one named ICP* who likes that outcome. (Brand = deliberate pairing through an outcome. Pair toward us, never with something the ICP dislikes.)
- [ ] **Educates, not entertains** — names the specific behavior/belief it changes. No viral-for-its-own-sake. *All views aren't equal; education viewers are our people.*
- [ ] **For-you, not for-us** — made for the ICP, not internal/team taste.
- [ ] **On-lane / narrow** — sits in AI-native engineering (or a *deliberate* adjacent widen, never a random one).
- [ ] **Clear, not vague** — hook says *exactly* what the reader gets. No curiosity-gap bait.
- [ ] **Proof · Promise · Plan** in the first ~3 lines (lead with **Proof** — Puru's ex-Google/FDE/Lex credibility — because the reader's silent first question is "why should I listen to you?").
- [ ] **Assume nothing** — identifier in the hook (role/credential > name), self-intro, why-listen, and **no unexplained inside jokes/references**. Every viewer is new.
- [ ] **SPCL present** — demonstrates **S**tatus + (**P**ower or **C**redibility), and carries **L**ikeness (sounds like the real Puru). Only make a request where Puru's SPCL is high (AI/eng — not off-domain).
- [ ] **Designed for the right metric** — built to earn **reach × right-ICP-engagement** (reshares / saves / comments-from-engineers / qualified DMs / opt-ins), NOT raw likes. *Likes ain't cash.*

### B. Format gates (our own data: 147-word NVIDIA post → 56k impressions; 369-word post → 350)

- [ ] **~150 words** (~780–840 chars), **8–13 short paragraphs**, 1–2 sentences each
- [ ] Blank line between paragraphs (heavy white space, scannable)
- [ ] ~35–40s read
- [ ] Lead with a concrete story/hook in line 1; **message > production** (clarity beats effects)
- [ ] One idea per line
- [ ] End on a punchy, quotable **mic-drop** line
- [ ] **Pair with an image** — order of preference:
  1. Clean photo of a **recognizable face** (Jensen drove the 56k winner; use the subject's face for person-posts)
  2. Screenshot with emotion (offer letter, Reddit/X success story, real product UI)
  3. Borrowed dev meme
  4. AI-photoreal scene with the hook painted in + brand logos
- [ ] **Verify** every public stat/quote before posting (state facts, tell the truth). Attribute anecdotes to the person who tells them — don't quote third parties verbatim.

## 6. The Repost Engine (how we actually grow followers)

**Reposts > likes.** Each repost exposes us to a NEW follower graph. Likes are vanity.

- Build **2–3 evergreen save-able templates** in our lane, e.g.:
  - *"If I were learning AI-native engineering from scratch in 2026, study these 30 resources: 1… 2…"*
  - *"The 12 things a developer must do to go from AI-ready to AI-native: 1… 2…"*
  - *"7 agent workflows that 2x'd how much code my team ships: 1… 2…"*
- Re-run each template every **2–3 weeks** (Sameer ran ONE template 9× → 8–148 reposts each time).
- Design posts to be **shared/saved**, not just liked: lists, frameworks, screenshots.

---

## 7. Hook Library (steal these molds)

- **Big-number + comparison:** "NVIDIA just did $X. Here's what most engineers missed."
- **Two-line contrarian flip:** "Writing code is no longer the job. Reviewing AI's code is."
- **Second-person cold-open:** "You're in a design review and the AI already wrote the service…" *(highest mean engagement)*
- **First-person vulnerable:** "I noticed a pattern at Google before I left…" *(drives comments)*
- **Salary/stakes anchor:** "The engineers who learn this will out-earn the ones who don't by 2027."
- **Question hook:** "What actually separates an AI-native engineer from an AI-ready one?"

## 8. Metrics — what we track (weekly review)

- **PRIMARY: reposts** (follower-graph expansion) and **follower delta**
- **SECONDARY: comments** (algo signal), saves, profile views, DMs from ICP2/3/4
- **IGNORE as a goal:** raw likes
- **Intent-mix audit (every week):** tally the week's posts by Intent and compare to target **Teach 45 / Provoke 30 / Prove 10 / Relate 10 / Reach 5**. Drifting toward Teach-only or Reach-heavy is the early-warning sign — correct next week's slots in §4. (Reach must stay ≤5% / capped; if it creeps up, RPM-equivalent quality is leaking.)
- Weekly: which **Intent / Topic / Format** / hook mold / image type printed reposts and right-ICP engagement? Double down. Kill what didn't.
- Expect a flat early stretch. Do NOT change the lane in response to flat weeks — only change format/hook.

---

## 9. Kill Criteria (when in doubt, don't post)

Fast triage before the full §5 gate. Kill a draft if ANY of these:
- Can't name its single ICP or the good outcome it pairs Puru with
- Has two ideas
- Doesn't teach, provoke, or prove (doesn't change a behavior/belief)
- Vague/curiosity-bait hook — doesn't say what the reader gets
- Missing Proof · Promise · Plan, or assumes the reader already knows Puru
- Off-lane or off-domain for Puru's authority (low SPCL)
- Over ~180 words or wall-of-text
- Opinion isn't genuinely Puru's
- No image plan
- Contains an unverified stat/quote

> **Don't get the same scar twice.** When a post under/over-performs for a reason, the team lead encodes the lesson as a new gate here or in §5 — we never relearn it.
