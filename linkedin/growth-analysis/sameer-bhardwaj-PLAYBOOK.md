## 0. The one-sentence answer

> He picked ONE narrow, high-status, high-pain niche (system design interviews at FAANG), posted ~6×/week for 11 months without ever drifting, and engineered a small number of **repost-bait resource posts that he recycles every few weeks** — because reposts, not likes, are what put him in front of strangers' feeds and convert to followers.

Likes are a vanity scoreboard. **Reposts are the growth engine.** Everything below is downstream of that.

## 3. THE REPOST ENGINE (most important section)

His single highest-leverage asset is **one post template, recycled 9 times in 11 months:**

> *"If I travel back to my Interview Days, I will study these 35 Articles to learn system design Fundamentals. These articles and videos will save you 50+ hours of hopping to resources and wasting time. 1. APIs: [link] 2. API Gateways: [link] …"*

Every time he re-runs it, it prints reposts:

| Post | When | Likes | Reposts |
|---|---|---|---|
| #182 | 7mo ago | 2,457 | **148** |
| #99 | 4mo ago | 1,487 | **114** |
| #73 | 3mo ago | 1,152 | **142** |
| #43 | 1mo ago | 773 | 88 |
| #153 | 6mo ago | 735 | 70 |
| #24 | 1mo ago | 563 | 51 |
| …9 total | | 234–2,457 | 8–148 |

**Why this works (the psychology):**
1. **Save-ability** — a numbered link list is a resource you *keep*. Reposting = bookmarking publicly.
2. **Cheap altruism** — resharing it makes the resharer look helpful to *their* network at zero effort/risk. Reposting an opinion is risky (it's a stance); resharing a free resource list is pure upside.
3. **Each repost is a free ad** — it exposes him to the resharer's entire follower graph. 148 reposts ≈ 148 new audiences. **This is the follower-acquisition mechanism.**
4. The IMAGE on these is not the list — it's a **Microsoft offer-letter screenshot ("Congratulations on your offer!")**. The image sells the *dream*; the text delivers the *how*. Aspiration + utility = reshare.

> **Takeaway for us:** Build 2–3 genuinely useful, save-able resource lists in OUR niche (AI/ML system design, agent engineering, whatever we own). Post one. Wait 3–4 weeks. Post it again, lightly reworded, fresh image. Repeat. This is not spam — at his scale most of the audience is new each time, and the data proves it re-performs.

## 4. Hook formulas (the first line is 80% of the result)

Median likes by hook pattern (first line):

| Hook pattern | n | med Likes | mean |
|---|---|---|---|
| Names a company in line 1 | 118 | 309 | 426 |
| Second person ("You're in a…") | 89 | 306 | **477** |
| Contains a big number | 95 | 275 | 429 |
| First-person ("I noticed…") | 24 | 268 | 446 |
| Question hook | 9 | 243 | **493** |

His repeatable hook molds (steal these structures):

1. **Big-number shock + comparison**
   *"Stripe processes 13,000 requests per second. Cloudflare processes 50 million. Discord 4 billion messages/day. None of them drop yours."* (#3, 401 likes)
2. **Two-line contrarian flip**
   *"DSA gets you shortlisted. But it's System Design that gets you the offer."* (#201, 1,968 likes, 140 reposts)
3. **Second-person interview cold-open**
   *"You're in a system design interview at Meta. The interviewer asks you a tricky…"* (#177, 1,325 likes) — puts the reader *inside* the scene.
4. **Salary anchor**
   *"Anthropic is paying $320k (~2.9 CR+) base to its software engineers…"* (#66, 1,230 likes) — money is the highest-arousal hook in a career niche.
5. **Both-sides / "everyone is wrong"**
   *"Half the internet thinks this AI is dumb. The other half thinks it's correct. Both are right."* (#2)

**Body structure he repeats:** Hook (1–2 short lines, each own paragraph) → the mechanism or story → **"what the senior engineer actually does"** reframe → soft P.S. CTA. The "senior engineer / what separates them" framing is his signature lesson-delivery move.

## 5. The image system (5 archetypes — none are infographics)

His images are not charts or stock photos. They are **screenshots of "real" artifacts** or **AI-gen scenes that render the message**. Dark-mode, mobile, high-contrast, thumb-stopping:

1. **Offer-letter screenshot** — "Congratulations on your offer!" Microsoft/Google email. Pure aspiration. (Pairs with resource lists.)
2. **Reddit/Twitter success story, yellow-highlighted** — e.g. an r/leetcode "13 LPA → 45 LPA" post with the key lines underlined in highlighter. Social proof + his thesis underlined literally.
3. **AI-gen photoreal scene with the hook painted in** — a boardroom with *"DSA shows your problem-solving. System Design shows your maturity"* on the wall + Amazon/Microsoft/Google logos. The text *becomes* the image. Reproducible with any image model.
4. **Borrowed tweet meme** (dark mode) — his single most-liked post (2,577) is a reposted tweet meme. Cheap, relatable, devs love self-deprecation.
5. **Personal photo with faces + brand backdrop** — him at the Google office. Used for trust/story posts. (Matches our own finding: a recognizable face drives engagement.)

**Image relevance verdict:** the image almost never *teaches*. It **triggers an emotion** (envy of the offer, recognition of the meme, trust from the face) and the *text* does the teaching. Most people build the opposite (pretty diagram + thin text) and wonder why it dies.

## 6. Why people engage — the psychology, by action

- **Why they LIKE:** aspiration (salary/offer), relatability (dev meme/pain), or "this made me feel smart." Low cost, high frequency.
- **Why they REPOST:** the content makes *them* look good to their network at zero risk → resource lists and "useful" carousels. Opinions don't get reshared; *gifts* do.
- **Why they COMMENT:** personal stories ("I quit Google", "I noticed a pattern at Google") and mild-controversy hooks. Comments are the strongest algo signal — he farms them with vulnerable first-person stories (#266 joined-Google note-takers: 57 comments).
- **Status borrowing:** "Ex-Google" in his headline + Google/FAANG names in every hook. He continuously borrows the credibility of brands bigger than him. We do the same (Ex-Google, Google Cloud Security).

## 8. OUR 30-DAY PLAN: 9,100 → 20,000

> +10,900 followers in 30 days is aggressive (he took ~8 months for a comparable jump, but from a colder start and without copying a proven playbook). Treat 20k as the stretch target; 14–16k is the realistic base case. The plan maximizes the repost engine, which is the only lever that scales to strangers fast.

**Cadence:** 1 post/day, 30 posts. Best slots for our audience (India + US dev): 8–9am IST and a second test slot 7–8pm IST.

**The weekly mix (repeat 4×):**
| Day | Type | Job |
|---|---|---|
| Mon | **Resource list** (the repost engine) | reach new audiences |
| Tue | Big-number/system-design teaching | base credibility |
| Wed | Salary/market hook (AI/FDE comp) | high-arousal likes |
| Thu | Second-person interview/scenario | saves + comments |
| Fri | Personal story (Google, Lex AI, building) | comments + trust |
| Sat | AI-news reaction (timely) | spikes |
| Sun | Meme/relatable (borrowed or original) | like-magnet, low effort |

**The 3 repost-engine assets to build first (this week):**
1. *"If I were breaking into AI/Agent engineering in 2026, I'd study these 30 resources first…"* (numbered links, offer-letter-style image).
2. *"35 system design + LLM-infra concepts every applied-AI engineer should know"* (link list).
3. *"The 20 questions a Forward Deployed Engineer interview actually tests"* (ties to our FDE content already drafted).

Post #1 in week 1, re-run a reworded version in week 4. Each is a follower printer.

**Image production:** batch-make images in the 5 archetypes (offer screenshots, AI-gen "hook on the wall" scenes, highlighted Reddit/X screenshots, a few face photos). Don't make charts.

**Daily ritual:**
- Reply to every comment in the first 60–90 min (comment velocity = reach).
- Repost-with-thought 1–2 relevant posts from bigger accounts to get on their radar.
- Track each post's reposts (not just likes) in a sheet; double down on whatever gets reshared.

**Leading indicator to watch:** if a post breaks **>15 reposts**, it's a follower-acquisition event — clone its structure within the week.
