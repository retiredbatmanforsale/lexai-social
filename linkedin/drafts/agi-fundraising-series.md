## POST 1 — The critique (the hook)  · ~165 words

Here's an uncomfortable question:

If promoting "AGI" raised zero dollars — would the top labs still talk about it the way they do?

I don't think so.

Right now "AGI" lives on the front page of every mainstream outlet. Not in journals. On CNBC.

That's not an accident.

"AGI" convinces venture capitalists, sovereign wealth funds, and public markets that they aren't funding a better chatbot — they're funding the ultimate destination of technology.

And it's the ultimate recruitment dog whistle. The best minds in CS, physics, and math want to work on the most consequential problem in history. "We're solving AGI" pulls them in.

Take the fundraising pressure away, and I think the whole hype cycle quietly collapses.

What's left is calmer. More honest. More like actual science — computer science as a tool to solve the deep problems of the universe.

AGI isn't only a research goal.

Right now, it's a pitch deck.

## POST 2 — What real progress looks like (the list)  · ~150 words

If you removed the fundraising from AI, the word "AGI" might disappear.

Not because progress stops — but because scientists would describe it precisely instead of selling it.

Instead of promising journalists "human-like consciousness," labs would publish progress on the things that actually matter:

→ Sample efficiency — learning hard tasks from very little data

→ Causal & System-2 reasoning — deliberate planning, not statistical next-token guessing

→ Symbolic reasoning — real mastery of abstract math and logic

→ World modelling — understanding physics, not just text

→ Continual learning — updating like a human brain, not a frozen snapshot

→ Compute efficiency — algorithms that don't need the power grid of a small country

→ Neuro-symbolic integration — fusing deep learning with classical logic

None of these make a flashy headline.

All of them are the actual mountain.

"AGI" is the poster.

This is the climb.
