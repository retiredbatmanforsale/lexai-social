# Post draft — "What the hell is a forward-deployed engineer?"

A bank pays OpenAI millions and says:

"We want AI to cut analyst workload by 40%."

Nobody in the room knows how.

Then one person flies in.

Not a researcher. Not a salesperson.

A Forward Deployed Engineer.

Week one, they don't write code.

They sit with traders, compliance, analysts.

They learn the workflow before they touch a model.

Then they build. Prototype. Ship to production.

And when GPT fails on a compliance report, that failure goes straight back to the researchers.

This role was born at Palantir, on one idea:

Customers don't buy software. They buy outcomes.

In the AI era, that idea is everything.

Models are becoming a commodity.

Anyone can call the same API.

The scarce skill isn't building the model anymore.

It's turning the model into a number a CEO actually cares about.

That's the whole job.
